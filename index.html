New York State Farmers Markets and Small Businesses

Do farmers markets affect the growth of small businesses?

Hypothesis

The amount of farmers markets per NY Metropolitan Statistical Area (MSA) leads to a rise in small business growth.

Introduction

Farmers markets have become more than just places to buy fresh food, they help local communities grow. In the New York Metropolitan area (MSA), these markets may be helping small businesses succeed. By giving local vendors a space to sell their products, farmers markets can support new and growing businesses. This research looks at the idea that more farmers markets in an area can lead to more small business growth.

Conclusion

The downward trend in the graph disproves my hypothesis, indicating that a higher number of farmers markets in New York does not directly drive small business growth. While some areas with many markets show strong growth, others, like Rochester, demonstrate that more markets can coincide with lower growth, suggesting other factors play a larger role in business development.
